# eTAX BIR60 — Filing the Partial Exemption Claim

Step-by-step walkthrough for submitting the DIPN 38 partial exemption claim through HK IRD's eTAX online portal (Individual Tax Portal, "ITP").

## Prerequisites

You should have completed:
- DIPN 38 computation done and PDF generated (using `scripts/gen_pdf.py`)
- IR56B from your HK employer (shows the total income figure including share awards)
- Login credentials for eTAX/ITP (HKID + ITP password or iAM Smart+)

The PDF this skill generates is the **primary supporting document** for the claim. eTAX accepts **one (1) PDF file up to 20 MB** as Section 4's attachment.

---

## Step 1 — Login to eTAX

Go to <https://www.gov.hk/en/apps/irditplogin.htm> → log in with your ITP credentials. Select "File Tax Return – Individuals (BIR60)" for the current year of assessment.

---

## Step 2 — Enable the partial exemption Part

This is the **single most common stumbling block**. By default eTAX doesn't show Box 34 (the "exclude" box) or the Appendix Section 4 form — they're hidden until you opt into them via the navigation menu.

In the left-hand navigation, find **`02 Select PARTS of Return`** (it appears between Personal Particulars and the substantive parts). Click it.

Inside, look for and **tick**:
- **"Apply for full / partial exemption of income"** (or similar wording — IRD changes this label between years)

Save / proceed. The navigation should now include a new section for the exemption claim, and Part 4.1's later boxes will become available.

If you can't find the toggle, look in the same general area for these alternative labels:
- "Application for full / partial exemption"
- "Section 4 of the Appendix"
- "Application under section 8(1A)"

---

## Step 3 — Part 4.1: Salaries income

Fill in the income boxes from your IR56B:

| Box | Field | What to fill |
|---|---|---|
| **30** | Income from Office or Employment | **Total income** from IR56B (this *includes* the full share-award amount — don't subtract anything yet) |
| 31 | Share option gain | **Leave blank / 0** for RSU/GSU (these are share *awards*, not options; reportable in box 30, not 31) |
| 32 | Bonuses, gratuities, etc. | Per IR56B if any |
| 33 | Commission, fees | Per IR56B if any |
| **34** | **Amount to be excluded** | The exemption amount (Net Amount HKD − Net Assessable HKD, integer using floor) |

Box 30 is the **grand total** including share awards; box 34 is the **amount to subtract** for non-HK source. The IRD then computes assessable income = box 30 − box 34.

---

## Step 4 — Appendix Section 4: Application for Partial Exemption

Fill the form:

| Field | What to fill | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Grounds for exemption | **Non-Hong Kong employment** | Selects section 8(1A)(a). The "non-HK employment" refers to the **grant-time** employment (per DIPN 38 ¶43), not your current one |
| Name of employer | **Current HK employer** (e.g. "Google Hong Kong Limited") | Must match the name on IR56B — IRD reconciles against IR56B records |
| Gross income from employer | **IR56B total** (including share award portion) | Same figure as Box 30 |
| Income to be excluded | **Same as Box 34** | The non-HK source portion to be excluded |
| Days in Hong Kong | Actual days of physical presence in HK during the YA | See "Days in HK — how to obtain" below; this is a separate datapoint from the share-award day count and IRD uses it as a sanity check, not as the apportionment basis |
| **Attachment** | **The PDF generated by `gen_pdf.py`** | Max 1 file, ≤ 20 MB, PDF/TIFF/JPEG |

### Why "Name of employer" is the HK employer (not the foreign one)

Because Section 4 is bound to BIR60 Part 4.1 (which reports IR56B income), the employer field must match IR56B. The fact that the share award originally came from a non-HK employer is established in the supporting PDF (Itinerary appendix + footnote on page 1), not in this field.

If you put a foreign employer here, IRD's automated reconciliation against IR56B will flag the return as inconsistent and slow down processing.

---

## Days in HK — how to obtain

This is the actual physical-presence day count for the year of assessment (1 April – 31 March), not the share-award vesting day count.

| Method | Cost | Time | When to use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Estimate from passport stamps + flight emails + Google Maps Timeline | Free | Minutes | If you rarely left HK (< 30 days out of HK) — just `365 − out-of-HK days` |
| Apply for Statement of Travel Records via [ImmD online](https://www.immd.gov.hk/eng/services/statement-of-travel-records.html) | HK$ 160 | ~10 working days | If you travelled extensively, or want IRD-safe documentation |
| iAM Smart App | Free (login) / HK$ 160 (formal record) | Instant (login), 10 days (formal) | The app itself doesn't surface a free instant travel-day report; it just shortcuts the formal application |

**Important**: this number doesn't directly affect your apportionment (which is done per-vesting-period in the supporting PDF, not by annual day count). But IRD does cross-check against immigration records, so be reasonably accurate.

---

## Step 5 — Review and submit

Before clicking Submit:

1. **Verify Box 30 − Box 34 = your expected assessable amount**. If the math is off by more than HK$ 10, something is wrong; re-check.
2. **Verify the attachment was actually uploaded** (file name should appear in the form). The 20 MB limit is generous but some browsers silently fail on slow networks.
3. **Click `Compute Estimated Salaries Tax Payable`** to see the estimated tax. Sanity-check this against your own expectations (the savings vs filing without the exemption should be roughly `marginal_rate × excluded_amount`).
4. Sign with ITP password / iAM Smart+ / digital certificate.

After submission you'll get an acknowledgement on screen and a confirmation message in your ITP inbox the following day. **Save these** — they're your proof of timely filing.

---

## What NOT to do

- **Don't use BIR-SP4**. The form name sounds related but BIR-SP4 is for fund Carried Interest concession (private equity / venture capital), not share awards.
- **Don't try to exclude the share award from Box 30**. Box 30 must include the full IR56B amount; the exclusion happens in Box 34.
- **Don't leave Section 4 blank** even if you've put the right number in Box 34. The Appendix Section 4 is what legally documents the basis of the exemption; without it the claim is incomplete.
- **Don't put a fake "Days in HK" number**. IRD has immigration data and will spot inconsistencies; use a reasonable estimate.
- **Don't file paper if you can avoid it**. Paper returns take ~6 months to process; eTAX is ~6 weeks. The supporting PDF + Section 4 form is equally accepted via eTAX.

---

## After filing

Expect one of these outcomes within 2–3 months:

1. **Notice of Assessment matches your claim** — done. Pay the tax per the notice.
2. **IRD requests further information** — typically wants your employment contract(s), full stock statement PDF, or itinerary records. Respond within the deadline (usually 1 month).
3. **IRD partially disallows** — they'll explain why. Common reasons: claimed exemption for grants after HK transfer (those don't qualify), incorrect FX rate, missing supporting docs. You can object within 1 month of the notice.

For audit defence, keep these documents accessible for at least 7 years:
- The supporting PDF you uploaded
- Full stock statement PDF for the year
- Employment contracts / offer letters (both prior non-HK and current HK)
- IR56B from your HK employer
- The eTAX submission acknowledgement
